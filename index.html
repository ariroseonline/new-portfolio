College

Studied Experimental Psychology, Philosophy, Music and one course in just about every department.

Read a lot of books, acquired writing skills and (overly) critical thinking, lived in Vienna, joined a fraternity as an exercise in investigative journalism, and made a documentary. The liberal arts are still alive!

How does this relate to the matter at hand: Understanding and solving business and programming problems is not nearly as forehead-bruising as dealing with Kant and Hegel. And music theory is basically like design with sound.

Joining the Circus

Created ads and brands. Learned coding, graphic design, motion graphics, and how to sell stuff. Made some off-the-wall projects.

Developed CSS and presentational JS agility by coding other students' custom portfolios. These were crazy designs with even crazier pixel-perfection demands across devices.

Internship

Learn the ropes of the advertising and branding busines at Anomaly. Worked on Budweiser and Converse accounts.

But I would be transitioning into building dynamic applications.

Consulting

Realizing I needed a deeper set of coding skills to accomplish bigger projects, I spent a year studying CS, backend development, and the Javascript language. I went through hundreds of books, video courses, and tutorials (this was before Bootcamps). I lived in Atlanta in a $250/mo apartment while freelancr consulting on UX, digital stategy, and web design projects, which helped me hone those skillsets.

I was even on a reality show.

Then a rare opportunity came up. To help start a school from scratch. I had to take it.

Avi Flombaum (Dean), Adam Enbar (President) and I (Campus Director) worked out butts off to prove that you can teach the foundations of coding in 12 weeks. We had 20 ambitious and brave students in the inaugural class. I was doing things every day that I've never done before. Employer outreach for hiring , conducting admissions for the next class , and establishing up a Marketing and CRM system for both . Also, functioning as programming and design tutor, event planner , brand manager , and social media manager and office manager, open to close everyday . A lot of managing, apparently.>

After a huge success and sigh of relief. , I realized I really wanted to continue where I left off on my path to mastering web development.

I built my first production-scale front-ends at Universum.
